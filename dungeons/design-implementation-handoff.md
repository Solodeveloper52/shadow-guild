# design-implementation-handoff

Use this workflow for disciplined, audit-friendly execution from intake to closure.

## Entry Criteria

- Objective is defined and owner is assigned.
- Baseline evidence exists or assumptions are accepted.
- Timeline and risk tolerance are explicit.

## Phase 1: Intake And Scope

1. Capture objective, stakeholders, and decision deadline.
2. Define scope boundaries and non-goals.
3. Identify dependencies and blocking risks.

## Phase 2: Analysis And Planning

1. Build working hypotheses and verification plan.
2. Select metrics and acceptance thresholds.
3. Prepare rollback/recovery options before execution.

## Phase 3: Execution And Validation

1. Execute tasks in small, testable increments.
2. Validate outputs against quality and risk gates.
3. Capture evidence for each major decision.

## Phase 4: Decision And Handoff

1. Publish recommendation with confidence level.
2. Document residual risks and mitigation owners.
3. Hand off actions, deadlines, and success checks.

## Exit Artifacts

- Decision summary and rationale log
- Risk register with owners
- Follow-up action tracker

## Common Failure Modes

- Scope inflation: split into staged releases.
- Unclear ownership: enforce single decision owner.
- Weak evidence: flag assumptions and run verification loop.
## Execution Governance

### Entry Criteria

- Scope, owner, and deadline are confirmed.
- Baseline evidence and assumptions are documented.
- Success criteria and risk tolerance are explicit.

### Decision Gates

1. Preflight gate: readiness and dependency check.
2. Mid-execution gate: quality and risk checkpoint.
3. Exit gate: acceptance criteria and handoff validation.

### Exit Artifacts

- Decision log and rationale.
- Risk register with mitigations and owners.
- Follow-up action tracker with due dates.

### Escalation Rules

- If confidence is low and risk is high, escalate before execution.
- If evidence conflicts with assumptions, re-baseline and rerun.
- If owner is unclear, pause until ownership is assigned.
